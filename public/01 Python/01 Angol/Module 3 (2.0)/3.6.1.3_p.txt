<h1>Slices ‒ negative indices</h1>

<p>Look at the snippet below:</p>

<codep>my_list[start:end]

</codep>

<br>

<p>To repeat:</p>

<ul>
<li><codel>start</codel> is the index of the first element <strong>included in the slice</strong>;</li>
<li><codel>end</codel> is the index of the first element <strong>not included in the slice.</strong></li>
</ul>


<br>


<p>This is how <strong>negative indices</strong> work with the slice:</p>

<codep>my_list = [10, 8, 6, 4, 2]
new_list = my_list<mark style="background-color:#e6f2ff;">[1:-1]</mark>
print(new_list)

</codep>

<br>


<p>The snippet's output is:</p>

<codep>[8, 6, 4]</codep>
<p style="text-align: right;"><font size="-2"><b>output</b></font></p>

<br>

<p>If the <codel>start</codel> specifies an element lying further than the one described by the <codel>end</codel> (from the list's beginning), the slice will be <strong>empty</strong>:</p>

<codep>my_list = [10, 8, 6, 4, 2]
new_list = my_list<mark style="background-color:#e6f2ff;">[-1:1]</mark>
print(new_list)

</codep>

<br>

<p>The snippet's output is:</p>

<codep>[]</codep>
<p style="text-align: right;"><font size="-2"><b>output</b></font></p>
