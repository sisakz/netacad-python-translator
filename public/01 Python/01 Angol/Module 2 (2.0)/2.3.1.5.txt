<h1>Operators: remainder (modulo)</h1>


<p>The next operator is quite a peculiar one, because it has no equivalent among traditional arithmetic operators.</p>

<p>Its graphical representation in Python is the <codel>%</codel> (percent) sign, which may look a bit confusing.</p>

<p>Try to think of it as a slash (division operator) accompanied by two funny little circles.</p>

<p>The result of the operator is a <strong>remainder left after the integer division</strong>.</p>

<p>In other words, it's the value left over after dividing one value by another to produce an integer quotient.</p>

<p>Note: the operator is sometimes called <strong>modulo</strong> in other programming languages.</p>

<p>Take a look at the snippet ‒ try to predict its result and then run it:</p>

<codep class="syntax-color">print(14 % 4)
</codep>

<br>


<p>As you can see, the result is two. This is why:</p>

<ul>
<li><codel>14 // 4</codel>  gives <codel>3</codel> &rarr; this is the integer <strong>quotient</strong>;</li>
<li><codel>3 * 4</codel>  gives <codel>12</codel> &rarr; as a result of <strong>quotient and divisor multiplication</strong>;</li>
<li><codel>14 - 12</codel>  gives <codel>2</codel> &rarr; this is the <strong>remainder</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<br>

<p>This example is somewhat more complicated:</p>

<codep class="syntax-color">print(12 % 4.5)
</codep>

<br>

<p>What is the result?</p>

<h1>Operators: how not to divide</h1>


<p>As you probably know, <strong>division by zero doesn't work</strong>.</p>

<p>Do <b>not</b> try to:</p>

<ul>
<li>perform a division by zero;</li>
<li>perform an integer division by zero;</li>
<li>find a remainder of a division by zero.</li>
</ul>
