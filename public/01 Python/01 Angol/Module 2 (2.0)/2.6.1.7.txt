<h1>Replication</h1>


<p>The <codel>*</codel> (asterisk) sign, when applied to a string and number (or a number and string, as it remains commutative in this position) becomes a <strong>replication operator</strong>:</p>

<codep class="syntax-color">string * number
number * string
</codep>

<br>

<p>It replicates the string the same number of times specified by the number.</p>

<p>For example:</p>

<ul>
<li><codel>"James" * 3</codel> gives <codel>"JamesJamesJames"</codel></li>

<li><codel>3 * "an"</codel> gives <codel>"ananan"</codel></li>

<li><codel>5 * "2"</codel> (or <codel>"2" * 5</codel>) gives <codel>"22222"</codel> (not <codel style="color:red;">10</codel>!)

</ul>
 
<br>

<p><span class="label" style="background-color:orange;">REMEMBER</span></p>

<p>A number less than or equal to zero produces an <strong>empty string</strong>.</p>
<br>


<p>This simple program "draws" a rectangle, making use of an old operator (<codel>+</codel>) in a new role:</p>

<codep class="syntax-color">print("+" + 10 * "-" + "+")
print(("|" + " " * 10 + "|\n") * 5, end="")
print("+" + 10 * "-" + "+")
</codep>

<br> 

<p>Note the way in which we've used the parentheses in the second line of the code.</p>

<p>Try practicing to create other shapes or your own artwork!</p>
